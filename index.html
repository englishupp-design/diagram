Course curriculum

9 modules of transformational practice

How people came to recognise the importance of EI and began studying it. The history of the term from ancient philosophers to the present day, the evolution of the EQ concept, key research, and the introduction of SEL programmes in educational institutions. The impact of EQ on different areas of life.

Richard Boyatzis's model of EI. Practical scenarios: how to hold a student's attention, work with "closing off" students, and cope with emotional exhaustion. The connection between EI and learner motivation, memory, and identity. The role of empathy, emotional resonance, and reflection as tools for the teacher.

Brain states that positively and negatively affect learning. Words, formats, and actions perceived as threats: pressure, criticism, comparisons, trick questions. How to work with a student whose brain is in a threat state. Coaching practices for communicating with parents and decision-makers. The link between EI and monetising a teacher's practice.

The "triune brain" and the role of each part. The amygdala and the processing of fear and stress — analysis of real-life situations. The prefrontal cortex, working memory, and neuroplasticity. Cortisol and its dangers. How positive and negative emotions affect the formation of neural connections and a student's progress.

What an ideal learning environment is and how to create it. Dopamine vs. cortisol — motivation and demotivation. Positive and negative emotional attractors. The "flow" state and brainwaves. The two brain states — emotional and rational — and how to work with each.

What a trigger is and its connection to past experience. Typical teacher triggers, techniques for immediate self-regulation, and long-term strategies for working with them. The SCARF model — teacher behaviour through the lens of social factors. How to give feedback and coaching practices for effective teaching.

The difference between triggers and trauma in learning. Analysis of real situations: how trauma continues to live in the body and affect the learning process. Techniques for recognising and responding to the situation — coaching, simulations, exercises.

Stages and symptoms of professional burnout. Emotional exhaustion vs. emotional resilience. Techniques for restoring emotional resources and burnout prevention through emotional hygiene.

Practical aspects of running a teaching practice: drawing up contracts, handling payment issues, setting your rates, collaboration options, and structuring package offers.

✨ The course includes: demo practices, brainstorming sessions, and group work
